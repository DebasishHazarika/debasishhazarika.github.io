My research focuses on understanding the formation and evolution of galaxies through resolved stellar populations, large astronomical surveys, and modern data analysis techniques. I combine observational astronomy, statistical inference, and machine learning to analyze massive datasets and develop scalable tools for astronomical discovery.

Research Interests

Galactic Structure • Stellar Populations • Magellanic Clouds • Nearby Dwarf Galaxies • Variable Stars • Time-Domain Astronomy • Large Sky Surveys • Machine Learning • Astrostatistics • Scientific Software Development ---

Current Research

### Variables in S-PLUS I lead the **Variables in S-PLUS** project, which aims to exploit the unique 12-band photometric system of S-PLUS to investigate variable stars. My current work focuses on constructing the first narrow-band Period-Luminosity relations of Classical Cepheids and producing reddening maps of the Magellanic Clouds.
---
### Nearby Dwarf Galaxies I am developing automated pipelines combining **machine learning**, **citizen science**, and **image analysis** to discover ultra-faint nearby dwarf galaxies in DESI imaging. This work also includes artificial galaxy injection experiments to quantify completeness and detection efficiency.
---
### Milky Way Structure My research investigates the three-dimensional structure and chemodynamics of the obscured Milky Way using more than one million variable stars together with multi-band photometric and spectroscopic surveys. I am particularly interested in identifying new low-extinction windows toward the Galactic disk and bulge.
---
### Machine Learning for Stellar Populations I develop machine-learning methods to estimate stellar atmospheric parameters and chemical abundances directly from multi-band photometric observations. These techniques enable efficient analysis of millions of stars observed by modern sky surveys.
---
### Observational Astronomy I regularly lead observing programs using facilities including **Gemini South**, **T80 South**, and the **Himalayan Chandra Telescope**. My observing programs focus on spectroscopic follow-up of Milky Way stellar populations and multi-epoch photometric observations of variable stars.
